I'm a software developer who builds mostly with TypeScript and Go. My focus is on backend systems and APIs, and I enjoy working close to the infrastructure setting up CI/CD pipelines and making sure things run reliably in production. I care about writing clean, maintainable code and designing systems that are built to scale. There's something satisfying about the behind-the-scenes work that keeps everything running smoothly, even if it's rarely the most visible part of a project.

Working as a Senior Engineer has taught me that the deeper you go, the more you realize how much is connected. That mindset pushed me toward building a serious note-taking practice using the Zettelkasten method a way of linking ideas across disciplines so nothing you learn is ever wasted. I read broadly and on purpose: software design, philosophy, calculus, and algorithms. Not just to keep up, but because understanding things deeply is what separates someone who can follow a pattern from someone who can reason through a problem they've never seen before. I don't have everything figured out, but I show up anyway. I believe that real growth happens in the unglamorous middle, not just at the finish line, and I hope the way I approach my work can inspire others to keep going even when it gets hard.
